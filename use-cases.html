What CIQ Measures

From Human Strain to Decision-Grade Insight

Most systems measure data, workflows, productivity, and outcomes. CIQ measures the human condition behind them.

It translates AI-led conversation, structured self-report, and biometric indicators into usable insight on burnout risk, cognitive load, motivation, pressure response, emotional strain, and performance capacity.

CIQ is not simply a wellness tool. It is a human-intelligence layer designed for high-pressure environments where fatigue, ambiguity, emotional strain, and decision complexity can affect performance.

Use Case One — Healthcare Physician Burnout

CIQ for Healthcare: Physician Burnout Visibility Before Crisis

In partnership with Mithra by ShelterZoom, CIQ brings trusted human-intelligence infrastructure to physician burnout assessment. The system combines AI-led conversation, burnout-informed scoring, biometric indicators, and a Single Source of Truth layer to help healthcare organizations detect rising strain before it becomes crisis-stage burnout.

Healthcare Intelligence Workflow

Protecting the People Delivering Care

Healthcare systems are under increasing pressure, and physicians are often carrying the emotional, cognitive, and operational weight of that pressure. Long hours, documentation burden, patient demand, emotional intensity, moral strain, and institutional fatigue can slowly reduce a clinician's ability to recover, focus, empathize, and make clear decisions.

CIQ gives healthcare organizations a structured way to see these patterns earlier.

Through a human-like AI assessment agent, physicians can engage in a private guided conversation that draws from established burnout dimensions, including exhaustion, emotional distance, cognitive strain, physical fatigue, and reduced professional satisfaction. This is supported by biometric indicators such as pupil dilation, blink rate, head tilt, response latency, heart rate variability, heart rate, eye movement, and vocal prosody.

For the Healthcare use case, CIQ is partnering with Mithra by ShelterZoom to create a trusted Single Source of Truth for physician burnout assessment. While CIQ measures burnout risk, cognitive load, emotional strain, and biometric indicators, Mithra helps ensure that the underlying records, assessment outputs, recommendation history, and decision-support data remain verified, traceable, and consistent across the system.

Together, CIQ and Mithra create a healthcare intelligence workflow where sensitive physician readiness data is not fragmented across disconnected tools. Instead, the organization can rely on one trusted source of truth for assessment history, AI-supported recommendations, biometric summaries, and longitudinal burnout visibility.

CIQ does not replace clinical diagnosis or medical judgment. It provides a decision-support layer that helps protect the people delivering care.
